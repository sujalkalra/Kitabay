Crime And Punishment.

Genre: Literature (psychological novel)
Published: 1866
The story follows Raskolnikov, a former student living in poverty in St. Petersburg, who commits a double murder in a state of moral and psychological turmoil. He justifies his actions through an intellectual theory of being "aboveboard," believing himself to be extraordinary and exempt from ordinary morality. The novel explores themes of guilt, punishment, redemption, and the human condition.
